Rewarding

Rewarding involves recognizing employees, individually and as members of groups, for their performance and acknowledging their contributions to the agencyâ€™s mission. Good performance is recognized without waiting for nominations for formal awards to be solicited. As such, recognition is an ongoing, natural part of day-to-day experience. In addition, awards regulations provide a broad range of forms that more formal rewards can take, such as cash, time off, and many nonmonetary items. Supervisors must make meaningful distinctions when granting awards. Award amounts should be clearly distinguishable between different performance levels that are fully successful or above.
