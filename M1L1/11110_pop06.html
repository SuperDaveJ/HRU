Position Classification

OPM issues classification and job-grading standards that provide occupational information and grading criteria for positions covered under the standard government-wide classification systems (i.e., General Schedule and Federal Wage System) after consulting with agencies. Agencies use these standards to determine the proper pay plan, occupational series, position title, and grade of each position. Classification is closely related to compensation in the federal government because, under the General Schedule, which includes white-collar occupations and the Federal Wage System, which covers trades, crafts, and labor occupations, the determination of a position's correct grade directly translates to a prescribed rate schedule for basic pay.
