How do I get credit for this course?

The Learning Management System will track your progress and provide course completion credit after you have completed every lesson in the course and passed the post-test with a passing score.

Do I have to take the lessons in order?

The course material is organized so that it will make the most sense to you if you start with the first Module/Lesson and proceed in order.

Do I have to complete the whole course in one sitting?

No. The Learning Management System tracks the progress you make in the course. Be sure to exit appropriately, using the Exit button in the course, and you should be returned to the place you left off in the course from your last session.

How do I make sure my progress in the course is saved if I need to quit and come back later?

Make sure you always exit the course by clicking the Exit button, located in slide-out menu that’s accessed by the button in the top-right corner of the page. Simply closing the browser window may not save your data.

How do I know what to select?

Selectable graphics are identified by a small image of a pointing hand in the corner of the image, and the cursor will change to a pointing hand when positioned over a selectable graphic. When a graphic is selectable, the cursor will change to a pointing hand when positioned over the graphic. Also, bold text is selectable and allows you to view more information about a topic. Always read the instructions in the prompt area at the bottom of each page to ensure that you don’t miss any information.

When I select a link or graphic, nothing happens. Why?

You may need to set your browser to allow popups in order for some information to appear. If you are using the Google toolbar in your browser, you may hold the Ctrl key and select the item at the same time, which allows the popup window to open. In Internet Explorer, you can enable popups by selecting Internet Options from the Tools menu, then clicking the Privacy tab. Keep in mind that many of the graphics are not selectable. The cursor will change to a pointing hand when positioned over a selectable link or graphic.

What do all the buttons do?

The table below describes the function of the buttons used in the course.

BUTTON FUNCTION
MENU Returns you to the Lesson Menu.
GLOSSARY Provides a list of terms used in the course with their definitions.
RESOURCES Provides a list of resources (web links and PDF documents) referred to in the lesson.
HELP Provides this Help file.
EXIT With confirmation, closes the course and tracks your location.
BACK Returns to the previous screen.
NEXT Advances to the following screen.
PRINT Opens a printable version of the instructional text in the lesson in a portable document format (PDF).
Can I read a transcript instead of listening to audio?

Yes. Any page that includes audio also includes a link to view the audio transcript.

Can I skip the questions in the course?

The Knowledge Checks are provided for your benefit so that you may practice the new information to learn it more effectively and to prepare you for the post-course assessment. You are encouraged to review these types of questions.

How do I find out more information about a topic or term?

Select the Glossary option from the slide-out menu to review definitions of terms used throughout the course. In addition, you may view a list of resources relevant to the lesson by selecting the Resources option from the menu. When you select the items, they will open Web sites or PDF versions of various reference documents. You must have Adobe Reader installed to view these documents; you may download the reader at http://get.adobe.com/reader/.

I’d like to be able to refer back to the course materials later. Will I have to log in to the Learning Management System?

You may save and/or print the lesson PDF for later reference. A link is provided on the first page of each instructional lesson. Note that the Knowledge Review questions are not included in the print versions. You must have Adobe Reader installed to view these documents; you may download the reader at http://get.adobe.com/reader/. Also be sure to save or print the course resources, available from the slide-out menu.
